Summary

Marcus Rashford MBE (born 31 October 1997) is an English professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and the England national team. A product of the Manchester United youth system, Rashford joined the club at the age of seven. He made his first-team debut in 2016 and has since become a key player, known for his speed, versatility, and clinical finishing. Off the pitch, Rashford has gained recognition for his activism, particularly his efforts to combat child food poverty in the UK :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Career

Manchester United

Rashford joined the Manchester United academy at the age of seven and progressed through the ranks. He made his first-team debut on 25 February 2016 in a UEFA Europa League match against Midtjylland, scoring two goals. Three days later, he made his Premier League debut and scored twice against Arsenal. Since then, Rashford has been a regular starter for Manchester United, contributing goals and assists across various competitions :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

International Career

Rashford made his debut for the England national team in May 2016, becoming the youngest English player to score in his first senior international match. He has represented England in major tournaments, including the 2018 FIFA World Cup and UEFA Euro 2020 :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
