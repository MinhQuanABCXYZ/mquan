Summary

Marcus Rashford MBE (born 31 October 1997) is an English professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and the England national team. He is known for his pace, creativity, and finishing ability, having established himself as one of the top young talents in football. Outside of football, Rashford has been a leading figure in charitable and social causes, particularly in fighting child hunger.

Career

Manchester United

Rashford joined Manchester United's youth academy at the age of seven and made his professional debut for the first team in February 2016. He scored twice on his Premier League debut against Arsenal and quickly became a crucial part of the squad. Known for his speed and technical skills, Rashford has been a key figure in Manchester United's attack, often playing as a forward or winger.

As of 2024, he has made over 250 appearances for the club and has scored more than 70 goals across all competitions.

International Career

Rashford made his debut for the England national team in 2016, becoming the youngest English player to score in his first senior international match. He has since represented England in major tournaments, including the 2018 FIFA World Cup and UEFA Euro 2020, where he helped the team reach the finals. In the 2022 FIFA World Cup, Rashford scored three goals, contributing to England's impressive run in the tournament.

Personal Life

Rashford grew up in Wythenshawe, Manchester, and has spoken openly about the challenges of his upbringing. His mother, Melanie, played a significant role in his life, working multiple jobs to support her family. Rashford's experiences have informed much of his activism, particularly in his campaign against child food poverty. In 2020, he successfully lobbied the UK government to extend free school meals during the COVID-19 pandemic.

In 2021, he launched the "Marcus Rashford Food Foundation" to further support his initiatives and raise awareness about child food insecurity. The foundation focuses on providing healthy meals and educating families on nutrition.

Charity Work

Beyond football, Rashford is known for his charity work, particularly his efforts to combat child hunger. During the COVID-19 pandemic, he campaigned for the continuation of free school meals for vulnerable children in the UK. His advocacy led to widespread support and earned him an MBE in 2020. Rashford has also worked with food distribution charities like FareShare to provide meals to children in need.
