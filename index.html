Summary

Marcus Rashford (born 31 October 1997) is an English professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and the England national team. Rashford is known for his speed, versatility, and clinical finishing. Off the pitch, he has been widely recognized for his activism, especially his work against child food poverty in the UK.

Career

Manchester United

Rashford joined the Manchester United academy at the age of seven and quickly progressed through the ranks. He made his first-team debut on 25 February 2016 in a UEFA Europa League match against Midtjylland, scoring two goals. Just three days later, he made his Premier League debut and again scored twice, this time against Arsenal. He has since become a key player for United, known for his pace, dribbling, and goal-scoring ability.

International Career

Rashford made his debut for the England national team in May 2016, becoming the youngest English player to score in his first senior international match. He represented England at the 2018 FIFA World Cup, where the team reached the semi-finals, and was also part of the squad that finished runners-up at UEFA Euro 2020.

Personal Life

Marcus Rashford was born in Wythenshawe, Manchester, and grew up in a close-knit family. His mother, Melanie, played a significant role in his upbringing, working multiple jobs to support Marcus and his siblings. Rashford has been open about the challenges he faced growing up in a low-income household, which has inspired his activism. Outside of football, Rashford enjoys spending time with family, traveling, and participating in charity work.

Charity Work

Rashford has been an outspoken advocate for social justice and is particularly focused on combating child food poverty. In 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, he successfully lobbied the UK government to extend free school meals to children during the holidays, preventing millions from going hungry. His activism led to widespread praise, and he was awarded an MBE for his services to vulnerable children.

Rashford has also collaborated with charities such as FareShare, a food distribution organization, to help feed vulnerable children and families across the UK. His efforts have inspired millions and garnered global recognition for his impact beyond football.
